01

Scene Schema

Every scene output from SceneForge maps to these fields. Required fields drive the Firefly prompt. Export fields populate the InDesign Data Merge CSV.

Scene_Number Required

Sequential scene identifier. Zero-padded to two digits so InDesign sorts correctly.

Example01 / 02 / 03 ...
Scene_Description Required

1-2 sentence narrative summary of the scene for human readers. Written in active voice.

ExampleDr. Chen pauses at the clinic entrance, her reflection caught in the glass door.
Shot_Type Required

Camera framing code (ECU, CU, MS, WS, etc.). Drives Firefly prompt prefix and slide layout.

ExampleECU
Mood Required

Emotional tone. Use 1-3 descriptors, separated by semicolons. These feed directly into the Firefly prompt atmosphere.

Exampletense; uncertain
Lighting Required

Specific lighting condition. Must match a term from the Lighting Vocabulary for Firefly consistency.

ExampleFlat overcast; cool daylight
Characters Required

Names or descriptions of every person visible in the frame. Use descriptions only (not real names) for Firefly prompts.

ExampleDr. Maya Chen; Patient (male, 60s)
Action Required

What is physically happening in the frame. Use present continuous tense for Firefly alignment.

ExamplePausing at glass door, looking at reflection
Setting Required

Location and environmental detail. Be specific: Firefly performs better with grounded spatial context.

ExampleUrban medical clinic exterior, city street background
Firefly_Prompt Required

Full structured prompt using the SceneForge formula. Includes shot type, mood, subject, setting, lighting, palette, style, and aspect ratio.

ExampleExtreme close-up, tense and uncertain, a woman in her 40s pausing at a glass door, urban clinic exterior, flat overcast daylight, cool grey tones, photorealistic cinematic, --ar 16:9
Alt_Text Required

Accessible image description for screen readers. Describe what is visible, not the intent. WCAG 2.1 AA compliant. Questions: jodonnell@bgbgroup.com

ExampleClose-up of a woman pausing at a glass door, her reflection visible, overcast sky behind her.
Color_Palette Export optional

2-3 hex values or descriptive color names for production continuity across scenes. Matches the Firefly prompt palette language.

Example#2C3E50, #BDC3C7, #ECF0F1
InDesign_Notes Export optional

Layout or production notes for the InDesign operator. Not rendered in Firefly prompts or PPT slides.

ExampleFrame with 3px bleed; caption below image

03

Mood + Lighting Vocabulary

Use exact terms from this list in the Mood and Lighting fields for consistent Firefly output across your storyboard.

Mood

Tense Anticipation, threat, or conflict about to surface. High emotional pressure.
Urgent Time pressure, crisis in motion. Characters in active problem-solving mode.
Hopeful Forward momentum, possibility. Often follows a turning point scene.
Melancholic Quiet grief, loss, or longing. Slow and introspective in visual pace.
Serene Calm resolution. Often used for aftermath, reflection, or healing beats.
Contemplative Interior thought, weighing a decision. Characters still but psychologically active.
Vulnerable Exposed, unguarded. High in patient-facing pharma DTC narratives.
Ominous Dread or foreboding without explicit threat visible in frame.
Confrontational Two forces in direct opposition. Tension made visible through framing.
Triumphant Achievement after difficulty. Used for resolution or campaign climax scenes.
Joyful Uncomplicated positive emotion. High energy, open body language.
Disoriented Confusion, overwhelm, or medical distress. Often paired with dutch angle shots.
Combine: hopeful; apprehensive tense; resolute vulnerable; urgent serene; triumphant

Lighting

Golden hour Warm, directional, soft shadows. 60-90 min post-sunrise or pre-sunset. Emotional warmth.
Magic hour Blue-pink gradient sky, diffuse soft light. Cinematic and transitory. Reflective mood.
Flat overcast Even, shadow-free. Clinical or subdued scenes. Reduces drama; good for realism.
Hard side-lit Strong directional source creating deep shadows. Dramatic contrast. Conflict or revelation.
Backlit / silhouette Subject dark against bright background. Mystery, separation, or iconic moment.
Practical light Motivated by in-scene sources (lamps, screens, windows). Intimate and naturalistic.
High-key Bright, minimal shadow, low contrast. Upbeat, commercial, or medical product scenes.
Low-key / chiaroscuro Dark field, isolated light. Moody and high-contrast. Used for diagnosis or decision scenes.
Neon-lit Colorful city-night ambient. Urban environments, consumer health, or DTC brand scenes.
Fluorescent / institutional Cold, overhead, clinical. Hospitals, labs, pharmacies. Reinforces medical context.
Soft window light Indirect natural light diffused through glass. Warm and approachable. HCP office scenes.
Warm practical lamp Incandescent or warm-tone source in-frame. Domestic comfort, patient home environments.
Layer: warm practical lamp; soft window light fluorescent; cool daylight
